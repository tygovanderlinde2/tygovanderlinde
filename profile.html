Hello and welcome to a page about me

I am Tygo van der Linde, I am 18 years old.

I have one little brother named Xavi, my mother's name is Sandra and my father's name is Peter.


I used to play football for the longest time, but I quit since it stopped being fun for me. I also started working a bit more around this time so for me, it was the perfect condition to earn a little more money.

All of this was still in Zierikzee, where I used to live untill I was 17.

A few years ago, we moved to 's-Heerhendrikskinderen. 's-Heerhendrikskinderen is very close to goes.


But since I quit my at the time only hobby, I did get kinda bored. Ofcourse, I was still working, but I wasn't always able to work. And that's when I discovered gaming.

It all started by me going to a friend's house and playing on a playstation for the first time. When I came home later that day, I almost begged my parents to buy me and my little brother something like "a thing to play games on."

My parents, reluctantly agreeing, bought us a wii and a few things to play with.

It wasn't really a problem at the time, since we weren't playing to much and we were both keeping up with work and school.

When I was almost done with my first year of middle school, a few friends of mine were talking about pc stuff.

After hearing them, I formed an interest in pc's and pc building, it wouldn't take long before I build my very own pc all by myself.

This, however, is where some problems started occurring, since I was spending a little more time every day behind my pc playing games.

Pc gaming didn't really inflict too much damage to my school carreer, but I'm quite sure I would have performed way better if I had never discovered games and never got addicted.

After a while, I started noticing that, even though I knew pretty much everything about the hardware of pc's, I was still clueless about the software side of things.

So I just needed to learn about this, no matter what it took.

After a while of researching the software side of things, I realized that I was having a lot of fun doing these kinds of things. Looking back, this is what made me want to be a software engineer.
